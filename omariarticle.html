Hard News Nuclear Warfare: The Ramifications

According to the Arms Control Association, the world’s nuclear armed-states possess a combined total of 16,000 nuclear warheads. The atrocious part about it is that more than 90 percent of it belongs to Russia and the US alone.

From countless battles to countless takeovers, nuclear warfare has always been an issue since 1945 when the first test was blown. Not only is it mass killing over thousands of lives, the radiation that stays in the air is almost worst.

I have recently interviewed a worker at the NNSA (National Nuclear Security Administration) through email and have asked them specific questions about nuclear weapons. I asked them four questions concerning what's in there weapons and the radiation in them.

Does this agency still manufacture nuclear weapons? If not, when did you stop? Do you have a suggested solution for the radiation that stays in the air after time? Do you have a suggested solution for supporting people affected by this radiation? Do you you agree that this is a serious problem and that it needs to be handled?

Unfortunately she responded with saying, “I am not sure what radiation you are talking about." A worker of the NNSA denying the effects of radiation in their nuclear weapons is very suspicious.

There have been places where nuclear weapons have been used greatly. I am primarily going to focus on a military attack and the Hiroshima attack. Both of those incidents caused terrible aftereffects and left a scar on not only the people, but the environment affected.

According to The Effects of Nuclear War, by its project staff, there was an attack on a range of military and economic targets using a large fraction of existing arsenal blasts.

This caused enormous economic destruction and disruption to the environment. Millions of cancer deaths and cases of genetic damage occurred. It was insignificant in attacked areas, but significant elsewhere in the world. Possibility of ecological damage.

This also caused unpredictable psychological damage to the people in the area. Many people were presumed to be “scarred for life” from the trauma they just went through. People in Hiroshima were scar as well from the bomb dropped on them in 1945.

The incident in Hiroshima caused major radiation problems to the environment. People say that they can still feel the horrible damage that took place today in their lives.
